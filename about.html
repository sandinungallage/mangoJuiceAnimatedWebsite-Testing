The Science

Why This Island
Grows the World's
Best Fruit

Sri Lanka sits between 6° and 10° North latitude — the ideal tropical band for year-round fruit production. The island receives two distinct monsoon cycles — the south-west Yala monsoon (May–August) and the north-east Maha monsoon (October–January) — ensuring some region is always in peak growing season.

The ancient irrigation civilisations of Anuradhapura and Polonnaruwa engineered over 30,000 man-made reservoirs still in use today, feeding crops with mineral-rich highland water that modern agricultural science cannot replicate synthetically.

🌊

Indian Ocean Influence

Surrounded on three sides by the Indian Ocean, Sri Lanka's coastal zones benefit from warm, salt-laden breezes that increase mineral density in coastal soils — giving our King Coconut and coastal pineapple a distinctive depth.

🏔️

Central Mountain Core

The central highlands peak at 2,524m (Adam's Peak), creating temperature gradients that allow tropical lowland fruits and cool-climate highland botanicals to grow within 80km of each other — unique on earth.

🌋

Laterite & Volcanic Soils

The island's characteristic red laterite soils are rich in iron oxide, magnesium, and trace minerals that are naturally absorbed by fruit crops — contributing to the exceptionally high micronutrient density in our juices.

2,500 Years of Knowledge

A Farming Legacy
Unlike Any Other

500 BCE

Hydraulic Civilisation

The ancient Sinhalese kingdoms engineer over 30,000 irrigation reservoirs (wewas) across the dry zone — feeding crops with gravity-fed highland water and creating one of the world's most sophisticated ancient agricultural systems.

200 BCE – 1000 CE

Polyculture Farming Tradition

Sri Lankan village farmers develop the "chena" cultivation system — a rotational polyculture method that naturally regenerates soil fertility without synthetic chemicals. The knowledge passed through generations is still practised by our partner farms today.

16th – 19th Century

Spice & Plantation Era

Portuguese, Dutch, and British colonial periods introduce new plant species — including passion fruit, guava, and citrus — which naturalise into Sri Lanka's ecosystems over centuries and develop unique island-specific cultivars with distinct flavour profiles.

Post-Independence

Smallholder Agriculture Flourishes

After independence in 1948, land reform redistributes estates to smallholder families. Multi-generational family farms become the backbone of Sri Lankan agriculture — cultivating with intimate knowledge of their specific micro-plots, soils, and seasonal patterns.

2024

Island Squeeze is Founded

Island Squeeze is established with a single founding principle: that Sri Lanka's extraordinary smallholder farmers deserve a premium buyer who respects their craft and pays fairly for it — and that Sri Lankan consumers deserve honest juice made the right way.

Today

Island Squeeze partners with 47 smallholder farming families across every agricultural province of Sri Lanka. Every farm is personally visited. Every crop is individually tested. Every bottle tells the story of the specific family, soil, and season that made it.

The People Behind the Juice

47 Families.
One Island.

Island Squeeze works exclusively with 47 smallholder farming families spread across Sri Lanka's nine agricultural provinces. Every farm is visited personally twice a year, with unannounced spot-checks between seasons. Every single crop is tested individually before acceptance.

We pay a minimum of 40% above prevailing market rates as a guaranteed floor price — because exceptional fruit requires exceptional care, and the families who dedicate their lives to growing it deserve to know their work is truly valued.

Origin to Flavor

What Each Region Gives Us

Every flavor in our collection has a home — a specific province, soil type, and farming family that makes it what it is.

Jaffna · Dry Zone

🥭 Ceylon Mango

Willard and Karutha Kolomban mangoes from the intense heat and ancient clay soils of Jaffna and Vavuniya. Harvested before 6am, cold-pressed the same morning.

Southern Coast

🥥 King Coconut

Sri Lanka's golden Thambili, tapped at 7–8 months by our southern coastal partners. Naturally isotonic to human blood plasma — the original sports drink.

Ella · 1,100m ASL

🌺 Highland Passion

Grown on volcanic hillside terraces above Ella. The altitude-driven temperature swing between day and night locks in a spectacular sweet-tart complexity.

Kurunegala · Highlands

💎 Ruby Pomegranate

Over 1.2kg of aril-only pressed pomegranates from Kurunegala dry-zone farms, where intense sun concentrates punicalagins to world-class levels.

Anuradhapura · East Coast

🪨 Wood Apple

Sri Lanka's iconic Divul — harvested wild from ancient dry-zone forest edges by Anuradhapura farming families with centuries of foraging knowledge.

Gampaha + Kandy

🍍 Pineapple Ginger

Mauritius pineapples from the Gampaha lowlands, blended with Kandyan highland ginger root. Bright, fiercely anti-inflammatory, and deeply Sri Lankan.
